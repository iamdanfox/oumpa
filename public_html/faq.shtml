What sports are involved in the Pentathlon?

The five sports in the Modern Pentathlon are Shooting, Fencing, Riding, Swimming and Running. The five events are each scored out of 1000 points and are done one after the other over the course of one or more days.

How much does the Pentathlon cost?

Modern pentathlon doesn't have to be an expensive sport at Oxford. You'll probably have the basic kit already- a pair of trainers, swimming trunks/costume and tracksuit bottoms.

Membership of OUMPA costs £30 for a year (payable in Michaelmas term) and gives you access to all the kit and coaching that you need to start from scratch in the shooting and fencing events. This also covers weekly coaching in running and swimming.

Riding lessons work out at £20-25 each. If you're learning from scratch it can seem initially expensive but beginners of recent years have seen real success from a few hundred pounds devoted to an intensive period of lessons prior to Varsity.

Other costs such as competition entry and 'stash' will vary according to your activity and taste.All in,a year of modern pentathlon is unlikely to cost you more than £200. Given that this is equivalent to the cost of an adult course in any of the events in the outside world, this outlay provides a comparatively cheap way to pick up and practise such a range of sports now. - James Corbet-Burcher, Magdalen

Is the Modern Pentathlon a Full-Blue sport?

A score of 4600pts or over at the annual Varsity Match secures a full Blue. A score of 3600pts or over would secure you a Half Blue. In 2006, four Full Blues and six Half Blues were awarded to members of the squad. Nearly all of these individuals were new to one, two or even all five events. - James Corbet-Burcher, Magdalen
